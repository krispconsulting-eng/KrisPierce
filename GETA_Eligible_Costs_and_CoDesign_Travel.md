## National Co-Design Program: Travel, Accommodation & Facility Costing

### Assumptions

- **Co-design in each state/territory:** 8 locations (NSW, VIC, QLD, SA, WA, TAS, NT, ACT)
- **Departing from:** Sydney (GETA base + UNSW lead)
- **Team travelling:** 2 people per trip (e.g. Project Coordinator or Info Lead + Lived Experience Lead or GETA parent representative)
- **Duration:** 2 days per location (1 day co-design workshop, 1 day travel/stakeholder meetings)
- **Frequency:** Full national round in Year 1 (establishment), selected states in Years 2-4, full round in Year 5 (sustainability/transition)
- **Participants at each co-design:** 10-15 people (families, carers, local clinicians, disability professionals, First Nations representatives)

## Key Observations

1. **Co-design travel is the biggest single addition** (~$160-200k). But it's also the most defensible: genuine co-design in every state is exactly what the grant guidelines expect for a national program, especially for First Nations and CALD engagement.

2. **GETA operating costs ($341k) are modest** for a 5-year national service. Most items are small and clearly eligible.

3. **The 10% vehicle lease cap ($254k) is not relevant** since you're using flights/taxis, not leasing vehicles.

4. **Everything in this costing is eligible** under the grant categories. The question is whether it fits within the funding envelope, not whether it's allowable.

5. **The co-design travel should probably sit under Activity 1** (consortium management, governance, consumer partnerships) rather than being a GETA-only line. It's a consortium activity - UNSW, GETA, SCN2A, FAST, and clinical partners all participate. Consolidating it strengthens the "national reach" narrative.

6. **Participant honoraria are essential and eligible** - consistent with Health Consumers NSW guidance already referenced in the AWP. Budget $50-100/person per session.
