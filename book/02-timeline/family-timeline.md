### Shaping questions
- What did your grandparents survive — war, migration, poverty, loss? Is there a family pattern of endurance the book can trace forward to you?
- Which grandparent are you most like? Who said so first?
- Is there a family story told at every gathering? Get the canonical version down — then note who tells it differently.

## Parents' early lives

- How did Mum and Dad meet? What was their marriage like from a child's-eye view — and how did your adult view revise it?
- What were their disappointments? What did they want and not get?
- What was money like growing up? What was affection like — spoken, cooked, worked, withheld?

## Family traditions

- Christmas / birthdays / Sunday rituals: what was fixed and sacred?
- Which traditions did you carry into your own family unchanged? Which did you deliberately break? Which did Will's needs force you to reinvent — and what did the reinvented versions become?
- What tradition died with Mum's illness? What new one grew in its place?

## The farm (if the farm is family history)
- Whose farm, where, what era? What did the farm teach — work, animals, weather, death, seasons?
- Is the farm still in the family? What happened to it — and does its fate rhyme with anything in the book?

## Migration / place
- How long has the family been where it is? Any migration story?
- What does "home" mean in your family — a place, a table, a person?

## Shaping questions — the long view
- What did your family believe about illness and disability *before* Will? Where did those beliefs come from?
- What did it believe about duty and caring? Who cared for the old and sick in earlier generations — and was it always the women?
- What silence runs through the family — the thing generations didn't talk about? Does this book break it?
