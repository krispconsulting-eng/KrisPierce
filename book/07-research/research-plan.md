# Research — collect the evidence

Memory is the draft; evidence is the edit. Everything gathered here
fact-checks scenes, recovers lost detail, and later survives a publisher's
legal read.

## Shaping questions

- **Where is everything?** One hour of listing beats ten hours of searching: which drawers, drives, clouds, and relatives' houses hold material?
- **What did Mum leave?** Letters, recipes in her hand, photo albums she annotated? Her handwriting is a primary source and possibly an artefact for the book/website.
- **Who else holds memory?** Dad, siblings(?), oldest friends, the first families in the charity — list the people to interview while memories are retrievable. **Priority: anyone elderly. Record them this year.**
- **Request the records early.** Hospital record requests can take months — file them in Phase 2, not Phase 4.
- **What does the SCN2A community archive hold?** Newsletters, conference programs, early forum threads?
- **Interviews to schedule:** husband (his versions of key scenes), Ella (her own words), Dad (Mum's early life), a clinician ally(?), founding families.

## Method notes

- Capture provenance on everything: date, source, who confirmed it.
- Contradictions between memory and record are not problems — they're
  material. Note both versions; memoirs are allowed to say "I remember it
  differently than the photographs do."
- Sensitive records (medical, Mum's) — keep out of the public repo; store
  locally/privately and reference by pointer here.
