# Recurring Motifs — the pattern map

Motifs are what make a memoir memorable: readers recognise them as they
reappear across different phases of a life. Fifteen identified so far.
Themes (`themes.md`) are what the book argues; motifs are the melodies that
carry the argument.

## The thesis (working — now the book's centre of gravity)

> This is a book about how one family kept reinventing itself every time
> life asked it to become something new. Each reinvention wasn't chosen —
> it was called for by what life placed in front of the family.

The reinvention chain:
Kris became a mother after IVF → a rare disease parent → an advocate → a
national leader → a founder → now an entrepreneur and author. Ella became a
sibling in extraordinary circumstances. David became a father navigating
uncertainty. Mum became a grandmother, then a woman living with Alzheimer's.
Bob became a husband caring for his wife, and a father supporting his
daughter.

Readers who have never heard of SCN2A finish the book understanding
something new about their own lives — because every family is asked, at
some point, to become something it didn't plan.

## The emotional spine: care across generations

Not one caregiving story. **Three.**
1. Becoming a mother through IVF.
2. Caring for a child with a rare disease.
3. Caring for Mum through Alzheimer's.

## The motifs

1. **The dream of becoming a mother** — IVF, wanting, anticipation. Hope; control vs. uncertainty; the first imagined version of the family.
2. **Nursing: learning to care before becoming a carer** — deserves far more space than a biographical fact. Did nursing prepare her, or make it harder because she knew too much?
3. **Will changed everything** — not because of SCN2A; because life divided into Before and After. The defining pivot of the memoir.
4. **Ella** — currently underdeveloped; likely to become one of the most loved characters. The emotional question: *how do you raise two children fairly when one child's needs are extraordinary?*
5. **David** — the work is always "our work," never "my work." How the marriage changed, decided, disagreed, coped differently, evolved over decades.
6. **Bob** — unexplored territory: the childhood relationship, becoming a grandfather, caring for Mum, life after she died.
7. **Mum** — an emotional anchor because of who she was *before* Alzheimer's. Readers must love her long before they grieve her.
8. **Becoming an expert without intending to** — consumer engagement methodologist, national advocate, founder, strategic adviser, systems thinker — none of it planned; all of it necessity.
9. **Building SCN2A Australia** — not "then I started an organisation" but *"what was the moment one family wasn't enough?"*
10. **The shift from fighting to building** — early years: getting through. Current years: creating (SCN2A Australia, National Rare and Complex Epilepsies Collaborative, consumer engagement frameworks, Caregiver Wellbeing Framework, national standards, strategic consulting).
11. **Professional reinvention** — krispierce.com.au, stepping into consulting publicly, three days a week at UNSW while building a business, IP over hours, work untethered from a desk (Tahiti, Hawaii, Mexico, Nairobi). Another reinvention, not a career change.
12. **AI as curiosity, not technology** — discovering AI, realising it could amplify impact, the founding MasterClass Executive cohort, imposter syndrome beside leaders who shaped Disney and TikTok. Mirrors the parent→advocate transformation.
13. **The farm** — Highland cattle, Rocky, Freddie. Not side stories: the places where readers (and the family) breathe. Emotional recovery points between hard chapters.
14. **Systems (the hidden theme)** — hospitals, government, charities, research, caregiving, consulting, AI: always improving systems rather than working within them. Possibly THE defining characteristic of the narrator.
15. **Care across generations** — the three caregiving stories; the emotional spine (above).

## Stories to recover (feed `../05-scenes/`)

- **Childhood:** first memories · family holidays · Bob and Mum as young parents · school · early independence.
- **Teen years** *(the story may BEGIN here — "around when I started going out")*: What was teenage Kris like? What music defined it? Which friendships mattered? What did she dream about? Who did she think she'd become? *Readers love meeting the person before life changed.*
- **Nursing:** the defining moments/patients that shaped how she approaches care.
- **IVF:** not only the treatment — what it meant emotionally.
- **Will's diagnosis:** the specific scenes that changed everything.
- **Ella's childhood:** the moments that only hindsight reads correctly.
- **SCN2A Australia:** the early days · the first family · the first success · the first setback.
- **Mum:** before Alzheimer's · first signs · supporting Bob · her final days · life after.
- **Consulting:** the courage to publicly back yourself after years of building other organisations.
- **AI:** the moment it stopped being another tool and became part of the next chapter.

## Motif discipline (craft note)

A motif works when it recurs *changed*. Examples to build deliberately:
- Hands that care: nurse's hands → mother's hands → daughter's hands → hands on a keyboard building systems.
- The phone call: IVF results → diagnosis → another family calling for help → the call about Mum.
- The farm as the still point each reinvention returns to.
- "Our work": the pronoun as the marriage's signature, traced across decades.
