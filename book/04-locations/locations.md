# Locations

Places are characters. Each significant location gets: sensory signature
(what it smells/sounds/looks like), what happened there, and how it changed
across the book's span.

## Home(s)

- List every home in the story's span. Which one is THE home of the book?
- How did the house physically change for Will — equipment, modifications, rooms repurposed? A house being slowly rebuilt around a child is a visible metaphor; walk the reader through it room by room.
- What does your kitchen table hold? (Most family books have a table at the centre.)
- Where in the house do you go to fall apart privately?

## Hospitals

- Which hospitals, which wards, which years? Name the one that matters most.
- Sensory signature: the corridor at 3am, the vending machine, the parent beds, the sound of the monitors. What details are YOURS, that only someone who lived it would know?
- Is there a specific chair / window / corridor you can still see with your eyes closed?
- As a nurse, hospitals were your territory. What was it like when your territory became your captivity?

## The farm — essential, not a side story

The farm, the Highland cattle, Rocky, Freddie. These are the book's
**emotional recovery points** — every memoir needs places where readers can
breathe, and hard chapters need the farm chapter after them. The farm may
also be the still point each reinvention returns to (see
`../06-themes/motifs.md`).

- When and why did the farm happen? Was it a deliberate choice of refuge, a dream deferred finally claimed, or an accident that became essential?
- Rocky and Freddie: who are they (introduce them like characters — because they are), and what's each one's best scene?
- What do the Highland cattle give you that people can't?
- What does Will do at the farm? What does the farm let Will be that other places don't? (Same question for Ella, David, Bob.)
- What's the farm's daily liturgy — the feeding, the fences, the weather? Physical work as therapy the reader can feel.
- Is there a farm scene that belongs beside the hardest chapters — grief walked out in a paddock?

## Schools

- Will's schools: the ones that fought for him and the ones you fought. Key rooms — the principal's office where a meeting went well or badly?
- Ella's schools: where her separate life happened. Sports days, assemblies — who attended, who couldn't, and what that meant to her.

## Holiday places

- Where did the family holiday — and what does a holiday actually mean with Will's needs? The logistics, the courage, the pay-off.
- One holiday place from your own childhood that connects to Mum?

## Conference venues & the advocacy map

- First conference you attended as a parent. First one you SPOKE at. The green room before that first talk.
- International travel for the cause? What it's like to leave the family to fight for the family.

## Parliament

- Which visit(s), what for, what happened? The contrast is the scene: the marble and formality against what you carry in from home.
- What did you wear? What did you say? What did they say back — and what happened next, truthfully: change, or a photo op?

## Mum's places

- Her house — and what happened to it as she declined. Emptying it (if you did) is almost certainly a scene.
- The care facility, if there was one: the day of the move.
- Where she's buried or scattered, and what visiting is like.

## Shaping questions
- Which location appears at the beginning AND end of the book, changed? (Strong candidate for the frame.)
- Which place have you never been back to? Why?
