# Kris — narrator and spine of the book

## Arc (the identity journey)

Dreamer → Nurse → IVF → Mother → Carer → Advocate → Leader → Daughter →
Grieving daughter → Founder → Strategist → **Still becoming**

The book's deepest question lives here: *who is Kris when she is not being
what someone else needs?*

## Shaping questions

### The dreamer
- What did you dream of before you dreamed of children? What happened to those dreams — abandoned, deferred, or transformed?
- What's the earliest memory of wanting to be a mother? Was it ever complicated — ambivalent, assumed, fierce?

### The nurse
- What did nursing teach you that later saved you as Will's mother? What did it teach you that later *hurt* — knowing too much, seeing what other parents couldn't?
- Was there a patient or family from your nursing years who you understood completely differently after Will? That's potentially a whole chapter.
- What's the moment you crossed from being staff to being "the mother" — the other side of the ward?

### The mother / carer line
- Where is the line between mothering and caring? Did you ever feel it move? Is there a scene where you were doing one and realised you'd been doing the other?
- What parts of mothering Will look nothing like what the outside world sees?
- What have you had to grieve about the motherhood you imagined? Be specific — which imagined moments (first words? school gate? weddings?) and what replaced them.

### The advocate / leader / founder
- What radicalised you? The single moment you stopped accepting the system as given.
- What did leadership cost you at home? Was there a night you chose the cause over the kitchen table — and one where you chose the kitchen table?
- Impostor moments: standing in Parliament or on a stage thinking *how did the girl from ___ get here?*
- What do people get wrong about you when they call you inspiring?

### The daughter
- When Mum began disappearing, you were already a carer. What was it like to run two caring lives in opposite directions — one child growing, one mother fading?
- What did you learn as Will's mother that you used as Mum's daughter — and what did NOT transfer?
- What did you never get to say to her? What did you get to say just in time?

### Strengths / weaknesses (be unflattering — the book needs it)
- What do you do under pressure that you're proud of? What do you do that you're not?
- What's your characteristic flaw as the narrator of this story — control? over-functioning? not asking for help? deflecting with competence?
- Where were you wrong — genuinely wrong — in this story? (Memoirs live or die on the narrator's honesty about this.)

### Still becoming
- Who are you now that Mum is gone and the charity stands on its own legs?
- What do you want that has nothing to do with anyone's care?
