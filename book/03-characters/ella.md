# Ella

## Arc

Little sister → Observing → Adapting → Growing → Finding voice →
Young woman → **Individual**

**Structural note:** Ella's story should become *increasingly visible*
through the book — a rising line. Early: glimpses in the corner of scenes.
Middle: her own scenes. Late: her own voice, possibly her own chapter.

## Biography

- Born when — and what was the family already carrying when she arrived?
- What was she like as a little girl, in her own right?

## Shaping questions

### The sibling journey
- What's Ella's earliest memory of understanding her brother was different? Has she told you? Have you asked?
- What did Ella learn to do too young? What did she never get to do because of how the family had to run?
- What did she get that other kids didn't — the gifts of this childhood, not just the costs?
- Was there a moment Ella protected Will in public? A moment she resented him? A moment both at once?
- What does the "glass child" idea (the sibling everyone looks *through*) get right about her experience — and what does it get wrong?

### Ella and Kris
- What has Ella seen you survive? What do you wish she hadn't seen?
- What do you and Ella fight about? (Real texture, not the sanitized version.)
- What's a moment that is purely mother-and-daughter, with nothing else in the room?
- What does she call you on? Daughters see the narrator's flaws first — she may be the book's truth-teller.

### Finding voice → individual
- When did Ella start speaking for herself about the family — school talks, arguments, advocacy of her own, or refusal to be defined by it?
- Who is she becoming that has nothing to do with being Will's sister? The book must show this or it fails her.
- What does Ella want readers to know? **Ask her directly — consider a recorded conversation as source material.**

### Ethics
- Ella previews her scenes and can veto them — confirm this with her explicitly.
- Would she want to contribute in her own words (an interlude, a letter, an afterword)?
