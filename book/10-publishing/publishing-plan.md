# Publishing Stream

Runs alongside writing from Phase 4. Goal: the book arrives in a market
that's been prepared for it, carried by an author platform that already
exists.

## Positioning

One-line pitch (DRAFT — iterate):
> *A nurse becomes the mother of a son with an ultra-rare condition, the
> founder of the movement fighting for him, and the daughter losing her own
> mother to Alzheimer's — a memoir about the families we don't plan and the
> people care makes of us.*

### Shaping questions
- Shelf test: which shelf is it on — literary memoir, health/caregiving, inspirational? (Aim: **literary memoir** that the other shelves also claim.)
- Australian story, global market: publish AU-first (likely) — which AU publishers have loved comparable memoirs recently?
- Is Kris the author brand, or is the charity? (Keep them related but distinct — the book must not read as a charity fundraiser.)

## Spin-off writing pipeline (platform builders — start before the book)

Short pieces on the book's turf establish the author with editors and
audiences ahead of publication. Each piece is also a deposit in the book.

### Article 1 (flagged by Kris): "The impacts of a caring role as you get older"
The ageing carer — pitched as an op-ed / weekend feature (candidates:
The Age/SMH, The Guardian AU, The Conversation, ABC News opinion,
The Saturday Paper; carer-sector outlets as second ring).

Angle options (pick one spine, the others become later pieces):
- The body ledger: decades of lifting, broken sleep and deferred check-ups — what caring physically costs, with the women's-health research gap underneath
- The economics: superannuation and career shaped around care; retiring into poverty as the price of love
- The succession question: ageing parent-carers of adult children — "who cares for Will when I can't lift him?" (NDIS/systems hook, highly newsworthy)
- The double shift at midlife: caring for a child and a parent while your own menopause goes unremarked

Shaping questions:
- What's the single scene the article opens with? (Op-eds live on one scene, same as chapters.)
- What's the one ask — the thing you want a policymaker or reader to DO?
- Data anchors needed: carer health statistics, unpaid care economics, women-as-carers ratios (research task — verify current AU figures)
- Timing hook: Carers Week? A budget cycle? An NDIS review?
- Byline positioning: founder of SCN2A Australia + nurse + mother — which order?
