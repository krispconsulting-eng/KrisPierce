# Publishing Stream

Runs alongside writing from Phase 4. Goal: the book arrives in a market
that's been prepared for it, carried by an author platform that already
exists.

## Positioning

One-line pitch (DRAFT — iterate):
> *A nurse becomes the mother of a son with an ultra-rare condition, the
> founder of the movement fighting for him, and the daughter losing her own
> mother to Alzheimer's — a memoir about the families we don't plan and the
> people care makes of us.*

### Shaping questions
- Shelf test: which shelf is it on — literary memoir, health/caregiving, inspirational? (Aim: **literary memoir** that the other shelves also claim.)
- Australian story, global market: publish AU-first (likely) — which AU publishers have loved comparable memoirs recently?
- Is Kris the author brand, or is the charity? (Keep them related but distinct — the book must not read as a charity fundraiser.)

## Comparable books (to research and read — build the comp table)

Candidates to evaluate, replace freely:

- *Once More We Saw Stars* — Jayson Greene (parental grief, love-forward)
- *When Breath Becomes Air* — Paul Kalanithi (clinician on the other side of the chart)
- *Any Ordinary Day* — Leigh Sales (AU; life divided by the unexpected)
- *The Year of Magical Thinking* — Joan Didion (grief as literature)
- *Somebody I Used to Know* — Wendy Mitchell (dementia from inside)
- AU carer/disability memoirs — research current titles and recent prize lists (Stella, ABIA nonfiction)

For each comp: publisher, agent, year, sales signals, what it does well,
what *The Families We Become* does that it doesn't.

### Shaping questions
- Which TWO comps go in the query letter ("X meets Y")?
- What's the white space — what does no existing memoir do? (Working answer: the triple lens of nurse + rare-disease mother + Alzheimer's daughter in one narrator, plus founder-level advocacy access: Parliament, research, systems.)

## Platform build (start early — platform grows slowly)

- [ ] Author website (kris---.com): story, book, speaking, media kit
- [ ] LinkedIn growth strategy — you already have professional standing; begin writing publicly about caregiving/leadership/systems 1–2× month
- [ ] Media list: outlets that covered the charity; health/carer press; ABC etc.
- [ ] Podcast guesting list (carer, parenting, health-leadership, grief shows) — before launching an own podcast
- [ ] Speaking: inventory of talks already given → keynote one-sheet
- [ ] Newsletter? (Owned audience beats rented audience at launch)

### Shaping questions
- What audience do you ALREADY have (charity community, professional network, media contacts, conference circuits)? Inventory it — you're not starting from zero.
- What's the boundary between charity communications and author platform — who signs off on what?
- Will/Ella privacy in marketing: photos or no photos? Decide once, in `01-vision` ethics, and hold the line in every asset.

## Launch (sketch now, plan in Phase 6)

- Lead time: publishers work ~12–18 months ahead; align with an awareness window (rare disease day? carers week? dementia action week?) without letting the book be pigeonholed by one.
- Launch assets from `09-deleted-scenes/`: extended stories, talk material.
- Events: launch venue with meaning (the hospital? Parliament? the conference?).

## Success metrics (define now, so "bestseller" is actionable)
- [ ] Agent representation within N months of querying
- [ ] AU trade publication (or deliberate alternative)
- [ ] Speaking engagements booked off the back of the book: target N in year one
- [ ] The real one: messages from strangers saying *"I saw my own family in yours."*
