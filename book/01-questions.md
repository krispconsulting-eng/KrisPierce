# The Interview

Answer anywhere, in any order, in any state of polish. Write directly under
each question, or dump answers in a new file and we'll sort them. Skip
anything that doesn't land — a skipped question is information too.

---

## Part 1 — Framing (answer these first; everything else hangs off them)

1. **Who is this book for?** Your kids someday? Other parents walking a
   similar road? A general reader? Yourself? (The honest answer changes the
   voice more than anything else.)
2. **Whose story is "our"?** You wrote "the story of *our* lives" — you and
   your partner? Your whole family? Who are the main characters?
3. **What's the time span?** "The last how many years" — where does the story
   actually start? Your childhood, or the moment your adult life changed
   direction? (Many memoirs open at the turning point and reach backward.)
4. **Why now?** What happened — or what's happening — that makes this the
   moment to write it?
5. **The one-sentence test.** Finish this: "This is a book about ______."
   Don't overthink it; give me three bad versions if one good one won't come.
6. **The feeling you want left behind.** When someone closes the last page,
   what do you want them to feel? (Hope? Understood? Braver? Angry at the
   right things?)

## Part 2 — The spine (the change the book traces)

7. Every memoir traces a change in its narrator. Who were you at the start of
   this story, and who are you now? What's the difference?
8. What did you believe back then that you no longer believe?
9. What was the hardest single day? (Just name it for now — we'll write it
   later.)
10. What was the best single day?
11. Was there a moment you almost broke — or did break? What put you back
    together, or what's still in pieces?
12. What are you proud of that nobody claps for?

## Part 3 — The chapters (raw material)

### Before
13. Where did you grow up, and what did that childhood teach you (good and
    bad) that showed up later?
14. How did you and your partner meet? What did you imagine your life
    together would look like, back then?
15. What were you *for* in those years — what were you building toward?

### The turn
16. If the story has a "before and after" line — a diagnosis, a phone call, a
    birth, a loss — what is it? Describe the room you were in when it
    happened. What do you remember with your senses, not your summary?
17. What did the first weeks after look like? What did people say to you that
    helped, and what did people say that didn't?
18. What did you have to learn that you never expected to know? (Medical
    language, systems, advocacy, grief, bureaucracy...)

### The long middle
19. What does an ordinary hard day look like — the routine nobody sees?
    Walk me through one, hour by hour.
20. What has this done to your marriage/partnership — honestly? The strains
    and the strange gifts.
21. If you have other children or close family in the story: what's their
    experience been? What do you worry they carry?
22. The charity (SCN2A) — what made you start/join it? Was there a moment
    where private pain turned into public work?
23. Work and money ran alongside all of this — university job, consulting,
    the juggle. Where does that pressure belong in the story? Any scenes
    where the two worlds collided (a work call in a hospital corridor)?
24. Who showed up for you? Name the people who became load-bearing.
25. Who let you down? (You can decide later whether this goes in the book,
    but the outline needs to know it exists.)
26. What did the systems — hospitals, NDIS/insurance, schools, government —
    get right and get wrong? Which fight taught you the most?

### Meaning
27. What have you learned that you'd tell the you of day one?
28. What's still unresolved? (A memoir doesn't need a bow on it — the honest
    open ends are often the best chapters.)
29. What are you hoping for now? What does "a good future" look like from
    where you stand today?
30. Is there anything you've never said out loud that belongs in this book?
    (You don't have to write it here. Just tell me it exists.)

## Part 4 — Boundaries & logistics

31. What is absolutely **off the page**? (People, events, details.)
32. Whose permission or preview matters before anything is shared beyond
    this repo? (Partner? Kids, now or when older? Family?)
33. Real names, changed names, or first-names-only?
34. Rough target: a short book for family (20–30k words), a standard memoir
    (60–80k words), or "let's see where it goes"?
35. How do you want to work? Options — pick or mix:
    - You answer questions in writing here, I draft chapters from them.
    - You talk (voice memos / transcripts dropped into `scraps/`), I shape.
    - You draft rough, I edit and structure.
36. Do you have raw material to mine — journals, CaringBridge/Facebook posts,
    charity newsletters, speeches, photos, medical timelines? List what
    exists and where.
