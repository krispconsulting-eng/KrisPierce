## Candidate structure

A common and powerful shape for this kind of story: open **at the turning
point**, then move mostly forward, reaching back only when the past explains
the present. Alternative shapes noted at the bottom.

### Part I — The line (the before/after moment)
1. **Cold open.** The scene where everything changed. In the room, sensory,
   present tense or close to it. No backstory yet.
2. **Us, before.** How the two of you met; the life you thought you were
   building. Written with warmth — the reader has to love what's at stake.
3. **The first weeks after.** What the fall actually felt like. What people
   said. What you Googled at 3am.

### Part II — Learning a new language
4. **Crash course.** Everything you suddenly had to know: the medicine, the
   system, the vocabulary. The indignity and absurdity of it.
5. **An ordinary Tuesday.** One unremarkable, exhausting day, hour by hour.
   The chapter that makes strangers understand.
6. **The two worlds.** Holding down work — university, consulting — while the
   other life ran underneath. The collisions.

### Part III — What it cost
7. **The two of us, under load.** The marriage/partnership chapter. Honest
   about strain; honest about what held.
8. **The hardest day.** (Question 9.) Its own chapter. Short, maybe.
9. **The people.** Who showed up, who didn't; what community turned out to
   mean.
10. **Fighting the machine.** Hospitals, funding, schools, bureaucracy —
    the advocacy fights and what they taught.

### Part IV — What it made
11. **Private pain, public work.** The SCN2A charity — the moment the story
    stopped being only yours and became something built for others.
12. **What I know now.** The letter to day-one Kris. Earned, not preachy.
13. **Open ends.** What isn't fixed and won't be tied in a bow.
14. **Now / hope.** Where the family stands today; what a good future looks
    like. Ends on the same image the book opened with, seen differently.

### Alternative shapes (if the above doesn't fit)
- **Straight chronology:** childhood → meeting → the turn → now. Simpler,
  slower to reach the heat.
- **Braided:** two timelines alternating (before/after) that converge.
  Beautiful when the "before" is rich; harder to execute.
- **Letters:** chapters addressed to someone — a child, a younger self, other
  parents. Very intimate; suits a family-first audience.

## What makes this interesting to others (working list)

- The universal inside the specific: any reader who has had a phone call
  divide their life in two.
- Rare-disease parenting from the inside — a map for families a few steps
  behind you on the same road.
- A marriage under sustained load, written honestly by someone still in it.
- The double life: spreadsheets and board meetings by day, the other world
  always running underneath.
- Building something (a charity, a community) out of the worst thing —
  without pretending the worst thing was a gift.
