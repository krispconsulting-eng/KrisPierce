The reality

Most days, no one sees what this actually takes.

Appointments, medications, paperwork, advocacy, and everyone else's needs, on top of the job or the household you were already running. Then there's what nobody asks about: whether you slept, whether you've spoken to a friend this month, whether you've cried in the car and driven on anyway.

📉

Your health is the trade-off

Family caregivers across all conditions report worse health now than caregivers reported back in 2015. That's not a personal failing. That's what the load does.

🌫️

Grief with no end in sight

Not one loss, but a chronic, recurring kind: grief for what was expected, triggered again by ordinary events, without a clear finish line.

🧩

Generic advice doesn't fit

"Take a bath" wellness content wasn't written for a life built around someone else's medical needs. It isn't that you haven't tried. It's that it wasn't built for you.

Questions

Before you start

Is this therapy or medical treatment?

No. This is a wellbeing and peer-support course, not a substitute for medical or mental health care. If you're in crisis, please contact a crisis line or your GP first.

How much time does it take each week?

Expect one live group session plus a handful of short activities from your personalised plan. It's built to survive a genuinely busy, unpredictable week, not to add another obligation on top of one.

What if my seat is sponsored by a pharmaceutical or industry partner?

Sponsors fund seats, they don't see your individual wellness wheel data or activity. What they receive is aggregate, anonymised impact reporting (completion rates, average wellbeing improvement) after a cohort finishes.

Do I need to be tech-savvy?

If you can fill out a form and join a video call, you're set. The assessment and plan run in your browser, no app install required.

What if I can't afford $97?

Apply for a sponsored spot. Seats are donated by organisations specifically for caregivers who can't self-pay, and the application is short and judgement-free.
