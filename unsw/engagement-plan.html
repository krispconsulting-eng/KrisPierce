Engagement plan — where we spend our time

A consumer-led engagement plan for Rare Diseases NSW. Classic stakeholder management says "spend most time on the powerful institutions." Rare Power flips that: invest deepest in building the community's own power, then deploy that mandate to move the few institutions that hold the levers — at state, national and international level.

The 5 biggest-impact moves

  1. Resource Rare Power as a real hub, not an advisory garnish — a paid, supported Co-Design Group + Consumer Lead with authority over all four spokes. Everything else depends on this.
  2. Guarantee consumer power on the Steering Committee — co-design is worthless if the decision body can override it. Resource and brief those consumer advocates.
  3. Stand up the Aboriginal Advisory Group early and self-determined — equity for First Nations, CALD and rural/remote is a named weakness; it can't be retrofitted.
  4. Seat consumer reps in every Working Group and as co-investigators in Rare Innovation — that's how the value survives contact with day-to-day delivery.
  5. Point the funding/policy asks at the frameworks that unlock money (National Action Plan, NSW Future Health, MRFF/NHMRC, SCH/UNSW Foundations) — via consumer-led delegations, with the 4:1 ROI case.

Staying true to Rare Power — the test for every engagement
