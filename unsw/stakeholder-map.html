Recruitment & diversity matrix

Stratify the lived-experience seats deliberately — don’t take whoever volunteers, and don’t let the most articulate or most networked dominate. Sample across every axis:

Disease type Role — patient · carer · sibling · bereaved Age — child · adult · older Geography — metro · regional · remote Cultural background First Nations Severity / trajectory Diagnosis status (incl. undiagnosed) Access & communication needs Socioeconomic status

The anti-domination guardrails

1

Broad, non-advocate recruitment

Reach through clinics, GPs, NDIS, community / CALD / ACCHO channels — then use stratified random selection to fill quotas, not first-come.

2

Cap seats per organisation

No single group or condition dominates the room or the agenda.

3

Pay participants

Honoraria plus travel, carer and respite costs — unpaid participation silently filters out the under-resourced.

4

Equalising facilitation

Small groups, structured turns, plain-language materials, Auslan / interpreters / captions, and a hybrid option for remote participants.

5

First Nations on their own terms

Pre-engagement yarning, community-controlled partners and cultural safety — not tokenistic single seats.

6

System & sponsors as witnesses

Bring expert and sponsor voices in as informants, so the deliberating majority stays with lived experience.
