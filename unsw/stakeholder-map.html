Stakeholder map — centred on Rare Power

Rare Diseases NSW is Australia's first Comprehensive Centre of Expertise for Rare & Undiagnosed Diseases — based at Sydney Children's Hospital, Randwick, and led by Sydney Children's Hospitals Network + UNSW, with South Eastern Sydney LHD and NSW Health. Its model is a hub-and-spoke: the consumer-led Rare Power Co-Design Group at the centre, four delivery themes as spokes, and the wider ecosystem mapped across state, national and international levels.

Mission

To support the holistic health needs of children and adults, and their families living with a rare disease and enable them to achieve their best possible health and wellbeing outcomes.

Vision

To be internationally recognised as a leading centre of expertise providing comprehensive, integrated rare disease health care — underpinned by innovative research, education, training and advocacy — accelerating discovery and translating effective therapies into care for all people living with a rare disease in an equitable, culturally appropriate and person-centred way.

State → National → International

Rare Diseases NSW is the report: a NSW Centre whose evidence is the national blueprint. The discipline — build the proof at state level; sell the proof nationally; benchmark internationally — with consumers leading the room at every level.

State — NSW

Operate, deliver & prove. Rare Power co-designs; the model runs statewide via kidsGPS/virtualKIDS. Every win becomes evidence for the national case.

National

Influence & scale. Carry NSW evidence (4:1 ROI) to the Commonwealth; advance the National Action Plan; build unified policy with Rare Voices Australia; share the model so others adopt it.

International

Benchmark & lead. EURORDIS, NORD, IRDiRC and the International Advisory Group — global best practice fed back into the NSW model.
