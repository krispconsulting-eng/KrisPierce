# What You Can Claim — Comprehensive Checklist (Australia, FY2025–26)

General educational guidance only, not tax advice — confirm specifics with
your accountant, especially anything marked ⚠️. Rates below are the ATO's
published FY2025–26 figures.

## The ATO's three golden rules (apply to every claim)

1. **You spent the money yourself** and weren't reimbursed.
2. The expense **directly relates to earning your income**.
3. **You have a record** to prove it (receipt/invoice — this is what Hubdoc
   is for; snap everything at time of purchase).

Extra guidelines:
- If a client/employer **reimburses** you, it's no longer claimable. If you
  receive a **taxable allowance** instead (e.g. travel allowance), you claim
  what you actually spent.
- Mixed business/personal use → claim **only the work-related percentage**,
  and keep a note of how you calculated it (e.g. a 4-week usage diary).

Key: ✅ claimable · ⚠️ claimable with conditions · ❌ not claimable

---

## A. Against your UNSW salary (employee deductions, personal return)

- ✅ **Working from home** — fixed rate **70c per hour** worked from home
  (FY2025–26). Covers electricity, gas, internet, phone usage, stationery.
  Requires a record of actual hours (diary/timesheet — the app's monthly
  routine is a good prompt to log these). Assets like a desk/monitor are
  claimed **separately** on top of the 70c rate.
  - Alternative: actual-cost method if you have a dedicated office and want
    to itemise — more records, sometimes a bigger claim.
- ✅ **Self-education** directly related to your current UNSW role — course
  fees, conferences, seminars, textbooks, journals, and associated travel.
  ⚠️ Must maintain/improve skills for the job you have now or be likely to
  increase income from it — not a pivot to a new field.
- ✅ **Professional memberships, accreditations, union fees, subscriptions**
  to professional journals/tools used for work.
- ✅ **Equipment** used for work: laptop, monitor, keyboard, office chair,
  desk, phone — work-use percentage only. Items **over $300** are
  depreciated over their effective life; **$300 or under** claimed in full
  in the year of purchase (employees).
- ✅ **Phone & internet** work-use percentage — only if NOT already claiming
  the 70c/hr fixed rate (the fixed rate already includes these; no
  double-dipping).
- ⚠️ **Car** — not the daily commute to UNSW. Claimable: travel between two
  workplaces (e.g. UNSW to a consulting client in the same day), travel to
  conferences/off-campus work. Cents-per-km method: **88c/km, max 5,000
  km/car** (= max $4,400) with a diary of trips; or logbook method for a
  higher claim with 12-week logbook + receipts.
- ✅ **Income protection insurance** premiums (if paid personally, outside
  super).
- ✅ **Tax agent/accountant fees** and travel to see them ("cost of managing
  tax affairs").
- ⚠️ **Overtime meals** — only if paid a taxable overtime meal allowance.
- ❌ Regular commuting, ordinary clothes, grooming, glasses/contacts,
  childcare, coffees/lunches, entertainment.

## B. Against the consulting business (business deductions)

Everything in the ATO's "wholly and exclusively for the business" bucket:

- ✅ **Software & subscriptions**: Xero, Hubdoc (if paid separately),
  Microsoft/Google Workspace, Adobe, hosting, domains, Claude/AI tools,
  password manager — work-use % if shared with personal. *(The app's
  Subscriptions view gives you this list automatically — tag each one
  business/personal at review time.)*
- ✅ **Home office running costs** — same 70c/hr fixed-rate option, or actual
  costs. ⚠️ **Occupancy costs** (share of rent/mortgage *interest*, rates,
  house insurance) only if a dedicated area has "the character of a place of
  business" (signage, client visits, not usable as a bedroom) — and claiming
  mortgage interest can expose part of your home to **capital gains tax**
  when you sell. Get advice before claiming occupancy costs; usually not
  worth it for owner-occupiers.
- ✅ **Office setup / equipment**: desk, chair, monitors, laptop, phone,
  printer, cabling, lighting. Small business instant asset write-off may
  apply (threshold changes year to year — check current-year rules);
  otherwise depreciate.
- ✅ **Phone & internet** — business-use percentage.
- ✅ **Professional services**: accountant/bookkeeper, legal, ASIC fees.
- ✅ **Insurance**: professional indemnity, public liability, business
  contents.
- ✅ **Bank fees** on the business account, merchant/Stripe fees, interest on
  business borrowings. *(Fee Finder view surfaces these.)*
- ✅ **Marketing**: website, ads, business cards.
- ✅ **Contractors/subcontractors** engaged for business work.
- ✅ **Stationery, postage, printing, small supplies.**
- ✅ **Travel** for business, overnight: flights, accommodation, meals while
  away, taxis/rideshare. Keep a travel diary for trips of 6+ nights.
- ⚠️ **Car** — logbook or cents-per-km (88c/km, 5,000 km cap) for business
  travel between client sites; not home-to-regular-workplace.
- ⚠️ **Self-education/training** that maintains or improves skills for the
  business's current services.
- ✅ **Super contributions** for yourself (personal deductible contributions
  up to the concessional cap — coordinate with UNSW employer contributions
  so you don't breach the cap; check with accountant).
- ❌ **Client entertainment** (coffees, meals, event tickets) — not
  deductible even when clearly work-related. Networking event tickets ❌.
- ❌ Fines/penalties, private portion of anything, grooming.

**GST note**: if the business is GST-registered, claim GST credits on
business purchases through the BAS as well — Xero handles this if the
transaction is coded with the right tax rate.

## C. Donations & the charity (SCN2A)

- ✅ **Personal donations of $2+** to a **Deductible Gift Recipient (DGR)**
  are deductible in your personal return — keep receipts. Check SCN2A's DGR
  status on the ABN lookup; donations to a charity that isn't DGR-endorsed
  are **not** deductible.
- ⚠️ **No material benefit rule**: raffle tickets, fundraising-dinner
  tickets, chocolates, auction purchases are ❌ not deductible donations
  (you received something). Some "minor benefit" fundraising-event
  contributions can be partially deductible under specific rules — accountant
  question.
- ✅ **Out-of-pocket volunteer expenses** are generally ❌ for individuals
  (volunteering costs aren't deductible), but if you're reimbursed by the
  charity that's fine (just not also claimable). If you invoice the charity
  for services through the business, that's business income + normal
  business deductions.
- The charity itself, if ACNC-registered, is income-tax exempt — its "claims"
  are about **GST concessions, FBT rebates, and DGR receipting** rather than
  deductions. Keep charity expenses entirely inside its own Xero org.

## D. The "don't bother" list (never claimable)

- ❌ Entertainment and social functions, even business-critical networking
- ❌ Personal grooming (haircuts, makeup, skincare)
- ❌ Glasses/contact lenses (even if you can't work without them)
- ❌ Childcare (no exceptions)
- ❌ Regular commute, everyday lunches/coffees
- ❌ Conventional clothing (only logo'd uniforms, occupation-specific or
  protective gear qualify; if it qualifies, laundry at $1/load work-only,
  50c/load mixed)
- ❌ Home loan **principal** repayments (interest only, and only in the
  narrow place-of-business case above)

## E. How this plugs into the monthly system

1. Every receipt → Hubdoc at time of purchase (photo or email-forward).
2. During the monthly import review, tag deductible transactions to the
   right category — the category lists in the app already map to the
   sections above (e.g. "Software & Subscriptions", "Fees & Charges",
   "Travel").
3. Keep a WFH hours log (a simple recurring calendar note works) — it's the
   one record the ATO asks for that no bank feed can produce.
4. At year end, filter each entity's Transactions view by category and hand
   the accountant a per-category total with Hubdoc receipts behind it.

## Questions for the accountant

- Instant asset write-off threshold for the current year and whether to
  expense or depreciate any big equipment purchase.
- Personal super contribution headroom given UNSW's employer contributions.
- Whether any home-office occupancy claim is worth the CGT exposure.
- SCN2A DGR endorsement status and correct receipting.
